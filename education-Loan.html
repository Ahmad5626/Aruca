Features

  1. Loan Amount
    • The maximum loan limit is usually up to ₹30-50 Lakhs, but this can vary depending on the lender.
    • The maximum loan limit is usually up to ₹80 lakhs (or more) for studies abroad, depending on the lender.
    • The loan amount can cover tuition fees, examination fees, living expenses, travel costs, and book fees.
  2. Eligibility
    • The applicant must be an Indian citizen.
    • The student must have been admitted to a recognized institution for a full-time course (undergraduate, postgraduate, professional, etc.).
    • Age eligibility is typically between 16 to 35 years, depending on the lender.
    • A co-applicant (usually a parent or guardian) is required, whose income and creditworthiness are evaluated.
  3. Interest Rates
    • Education loan interest rates typically range from 8% to 15% per annum, depending on the lender, type of course, and loan amount.
    • Some banks offer subsidized interest rates for specific courses or students from economically weaker sections.
  4. Repayment Tenure
    • Repayment can range from 5 to 15 years, depending on the loan amount and the borrower's ability to repay.
    • The moratorium period (i.e., the period when repayment is deferred) generally covers the duration of the course, plus an additional 6 to 12 months after course completion to allow time for job placement.
  5. Collateral Requirement
    • Up to ₹7.5 lakhs: No collateral is typically required.
    • Above ₹7.5 lakhs: Collateral may be required, such as property, fixed deposits, or third-party guarantees.
  6. Flexible Repayment Options
    • Postpone payments during the study period (moratorium period).
    • Easy monthly installments (EMIs) after completion of the course.
    • Some loans offer a flexible repayment schedule, allowing students to adjust payments based on their financial situation.
  7. Loan Disbursement
    • The loan is typically disbursed in installments:
    • Tuition fees are directly disbursed to the institution.
    • Living expenses or travel costs may be disbursed to the student’s account.
  8. Quick Loan Processing
    • The loan approval and processing time are generally fast, especially if all documents are in place, typically taking 7–15 days.
  9. No Prepayment Penalty
    • Most lenders do not charge a penalty for prepayment or foreclosure of the loan, which can be beneficial for students who want to pay off the loan early.
  10. Eligibility for Moratorium Period
    • The loan allows a moratorium period during which no EMI is required. This period usually covers the duration of the course plus an additional grace period after completion of the course to find employment.
  11. Loan Purpose
    • The education loan can be used for a variety of educational expenses, such as:
    • Tuition fees
    • Books and study material
    • Accommodation or hostel fees
    • Examination fees
    • Travel expenses (for overseas education)
    • Laptop or other study-related equipment
  12. Wide Range of Courses Covered
    • Education loans are available for a variety of undergraduate, postgraduate, and professional courses including those in engineering, medicine, law, management, and arts, among others.
    • Both domestic and international studies are eligible for loans.
  13. Loan Transfer and Top-Up
    • In some cases, you can transfer your education loan from one lender to another, or you may be able to top-up the loan in case the initial amount is insufficient to cover additional costs.

Eligibility

  1. Nationality
    • The applicant must be an Indian citizen.
  2. Age Limit
    • The applicant must generally be between 16 to 40 years of age.
    • For undergraduate courses, the applicant should be at least 16 years old.
    • For postgraduate courses, applicants up to 40 years are usually eligible.
    • The age range can vary based on the lender’s policy and the type of course.
  3. Course Eligibility
    • The loan must be for a full-time course (undergraduate, postgraduate, diploma, or professional course).
    • The course must be offered by a recognized educational institution in India or abroad.
    • Recognized courses include engineering, medical, law, management, computer science, humanities, etc.
    • Vocational and diploma courses may also be eligible in certain cases.
  4. Institution/University
    • The institution/university where the student has been admitted must be recognized by the government or relevant education bodies (UGC, AICTE, etc.).
    • The institution can either be in India or abroad (international education loans).
    • The institution's accreditation and reputation may influence loan approval and terms.
  5. Admission Confirmation

    The applicant must have secured admission to the course they plan to pursue. This can be through either a merit-based selection or entrance exams. A letter of admission from the institution will typically be required.

  6. Co-Applicant Requirement

    A co-applicant (usually a parent or guardian) is typically required for the loan. The co-applicant’s income and creditworthiness will be assessed to determine loan eligibility. Co-applicants may need to provide proof of income (salary slips, business income details, tax returns) and identification documents.

  7. Academic Qualifications

    The applicant must have a strong academic record. Previous qualifications, such as 12th grade marks for undergraduate courses or undergraduate degree for postgraduate courses, may be considered during eligibility assessment. A good academic track record indicates the student’s potential to successfully complete the course and repay the loan.

  8. Income Criteria

    Lenders assess the income of the co-applicant (usually parents or guardians) to ensure the repayment capacity. Salaried individuals typically need to provide salary slips, bank statements, or income tax returns (ITR). Self-employed individuals need to submit ITR and proof of business or professional income.

  9. Credit Score
    • A good credit score (typically above 650-700) of the borrower or co-applicant helps in securing favorable loan terms, like lower interest rates.
    • A poor credit score may result in a higher interest rate or even rejection of the loan application.
  10. Loan Amount

    For loans up to ₹7.5 lakhs, no collateral may be required, but the eligibility is based on the student’s academic profile and co-applicant’s income. For loans above ₹7.5 lakhs, collateral (such as property, fixed deposits, etc.) may be required to secure the loan. The loan amount generally covers tuition fees, accommodation, books, and other educational expenses.

  11. No Age Limit for Parents/Co-applicant

    There is usually no age limit for the co-applicant (parent or guardian), but the co-applicant must demonstrate the ability to repay the loan until the end of the loan term.

Documents Required

  1. Documents Required for the Student
    • Identity Proof:
      • Aadhaar card (preferred)
      • Passport
      • Voter ID
      • PAN card
    • Address Proof:
      • Aadhaar card (preferred)
      • Passport
      • Utility bills (electricity, water, gas)
      • Rent agreement or Bank statement
    • Proof of Admission:
      • Admission letter from the recognized educational institution where the student has secured a seat
      • Fee receipt (if available)
      • Course details such as the course name, duration, and the institution's name
    • Educational Qualification Documents:
      • Mark sheets or certificates from the last qualifying exam (e.g., Class 12th mark sheet for undergraduate courses, and a bachelor's degree certificate for postgraduate courses)
      • Previous academic records and transcripts (for higher education)
      • Entrance exam scores (if applicable for certain courses)
    • Passport-size Photographs:
      • 2-3 passport-sized photographs of the student for documentation and processing purposes
    • Visa (for Overseas Education Loans):
      • If applying for an education loan for studies abroad, a valid student visa may be required (after approval of the loan but before disbursement)
      • Application form for visa (if applicable)
  2. Documents Required for the Co-Applicant (Parent or Guardian)
    • Identity Proof:
      • Aadhaar card (preferred)
      • PAN card
      • Passport
      • Voter ID
    • Address Proof:
      • Aadhaar card or Passport
      • Utility bills (electricity, water, gas)
      • Bank statements
      • Rent agreement (if applicable)
    • Income Proof:
      • For Salaried Co-Applicants:
        • Salary slips (usually for the last 3 months)
        • Form 16 or ITR (Income Tax Return) for the last 2 years
        • Bank statements for the last 6 months (showing salary credits)
      • For Self-Employed Co-Applicants:
        • Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 2 to 3 years
        • Profit and Loss statement and Balance Sheet (for business owners)
        • Business registration proof (if applicable)
        • Bank statements for the last 6 months showing income transactions
    • Collateral Documents (if required):
      • Property documents (if the loan amount is above ₹7.5 lakhs and collateral is required)
      • Title deed of the property
      • Property tax receipt or proof of ownership
      • Legal documents (if the property is mortgaged)
      • Valuation report (in case of immovable property used as collateral)
    • Photographs:
      • 2-3 passport-sized photographs of the co-applicant
    • Course Fee Structure:
      • A detailed fee structure from the educational institution indicating the tuition fees, hostel fees, and other expenses
    • Bank Account Details:
      • Bank account statements (for both student and co-applicant) for the last 6 months
    • Bank Account Passbook:
      • Passbook copy of the student's and co-applicant's bank account to verify their financial history

FAQ's

  • What is an education loan?

    An education loan is a financial product offered by banks or financial institutions to students to help cover the cost of their higher education. It can be used to pay for tuition fees, living expenses, books, and other related costs.

  • Who is eligible for an education loan?

    Eligibility criteria vary depending on the lender, but generally, the borrower must meet the following conditions:
    ✔️ The student must be an Indian citizen.
    ✔️ The student must have secured admission to a recognized educational institution in India or abroad.
    ✔️The student must have a good academic track record.
    ✔️ The student’s age is typically between 18-35 years.
    ✔️The student may need a co-borrower (usually a parent/guardian) who meets certain income criteria.

  • What expenses can be covered by an education loan?

    • An education loan can typically cover:
    ✔️ Tuition fees
    ✔️ Hostel/lodging charges
    ✔️ Exam fees
    ✔️ Travel expenses (for international education)
    ✔️ Purchase of books, study materials, and equipment
    ✔️ Miscellaneous expenses related to education

  • What is the maximum loan amount that can be availed?

    Please refer to our Eligibility section.

  • What is the interest rate for education loans?

    Interest rates on education loans vary based on the bank or financial institution. Generally, the rates range between 7% to 15% per annum. Public sector banks usually offer lower interest rates compared to private banks.

  • Is collateral required for an education loan?


    ✔️ For loans up to ₹7.5 lakh: No collateral is required.
    ✔️ For loans above ₹7.5 lakh: Collateral (such as property, fixed deposits, or other assets) may be required.

  • What is the repayment tenure for education loans?

    The repayment tenure varies depending on the amount borrowed, but typically, the repayment period is between 5 and 15 years. For loans for higher amounts, the repayment period may be longer.

  • What is the moratorium period for education loans?

    The moratorium period is the time frame during which the student is not required to repay the loan. It typically includes: The course duration. An additional 6–12 months after completing the course (to provide time for employment). During this period, the interest may either be accrued and added to the loan amount or be paid periodically, depending on the lender’s terms.

  • Can I apply for an education loan without a co-borrower?

    In most cases, a co-borrower (usually a parent or guardian) is required for the loan, as the student is typically considered a minor or does not have a regular source of income. However, some private lenders may offer loans without a co-borrower if the student has a strong academic background or a guarantor.

  • What documents are required to apply for an education loan?

    • Commonly required documents include:
    ✔️ Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, passport, etc.)
    ✔️ Proof of address (electricity bill, rent agreement, etc.)
    ✔️ Proof of admission (offer letter from the educational institution)
    ✔️ Academic records (marksheets, certificates)
    ✔️ Co-borrower’s income proof (salary slips, IT returns, etc.)
    ✔️ Collateral documents (if applicable)

  • How do I apply for an education loan?

    • You can apply for an education loan through:
    ✔️ Direct application at the bank or financial institution.
    ✔️ Online applications through the lender’s website.
    ✔️ Third-party online platforms that help compare education loan offers.

  • Can education loans be repaid early or refinanced?

    Yes, most education loans can be repaid before the scheduled tenure without any penalty. Additionally, refinancing options are available at some banks if the borrower wishes to secure a better interest rate during the repayment period.

  • What happens if I default on an education loan?

    • If you default on the loan:
    ✔️ The bank may report the missed payments to credit bureaus, impacting your credit score.
    ✔️ The bank may initiate legal action for recovery of the loan.
    ✔️ Collateral may be seized if you have provided any for the loan.

  • Are there any tax benefits for education loans?

    Yes, under Section 80E of the Income Tax Act, interest paid on education loans is eligible for tax deductions. This applies only to loans taken for higher education and the deduction is available for a maximum of 8 years or until the interest is paid, whichever is earlier.

  • Can education loans be transferred to another bank?

    Yes, many banks allow the transfer of an education loan from one bank to another. This is usually done to take advantage of lower interest rates or better repayment terms. However, the process may involve some paperwork and approval from both the current and the new lender.
